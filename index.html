The Core Story

Alex Rainer searches for his sister after the Helix virus mutates humans into Echoforms ‚Äî twisted remnants of who they once were. Each Echoform type reflects a different stage of mutation, memory decay, and exposure to radiation. Throughout the game‚Äôs journey across the Fringe, Deadlight Fields, and the Core, Alex encounters these creatures in ways that shape the story, the choices he makes, and the emotional weight of every step forward. Their behaviours, sounds, and environments tell as much of the narrative as the characters themselves.

In the Fringe, Alex encounters the earliest and least stable mutations: Lurkers, Shriekers, and Wailers. These Echoforms still cling to small fragments of humanity ‚Äî a twitch, a whisper, or a broken attempt at speech. They are unpredictable, often acting out of fear rather than aggression. Environmental storytelling shows their struggle: claw marks on walls where they tried to escape, abandoned camps torn apart from within, and faint echoes of memories drifting through their cries. Their presence sets the tone for the world: unsettling, tragic, and deeply personal.

As Alex crosses into the Deadlight Fields, the Echoforms become far more dangerous. Bleachers, Striders, Sirens, and the haunting collective known as The Choir dominate the landscape. Some Echoforms here move with eerie precision, as if still following routines from their past lives ‚Äî patrolling, guarding, or repeating broken daily habits.
